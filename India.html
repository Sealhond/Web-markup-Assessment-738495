Indian cinema, with its roots tracing back to the early 20th century, has evolved into a vibrant industry known for its diverse storytelling and rich cultural heritage. Bollywood, the Hindi- language film industry, has produced iconic directors like Raj Kapoor and more recently, S.S. Rajamouli, whose epic films like “Baahubali” have garnered both national and international acclaim. Comedy has played a vital role in Indian cinema, with classic films featuring hits like “3 Idiots” highlighting humor as a way to address social issues and cultural nuances. From the colorful musicals of Bollywood to the gritty realism of independent films, Indian cinema continues to captivate audiences worldwide, reflecting the country’s complexities and aspirations while using comedy to bridge gaps and provoke thought.

Various films:

  • Based on the true story of IPS officer Manoj Kumar Sharma, “12th Fail” sheds limelight on fearlessly embracing the idea of restarting the academic journey despite the setbacks and challenges and reclaiming one’s destiny at a place where millions of students attempt the world’s toughest competitive exam: UPSC.
  • I also highly recommend “Like Stars on Earth”: Ishaan Awasthi is an eight-year-old whose world is filled with wonders that no one else seems to appreciate. Colours, fish, dogs, and kites don’t seem important to the adults, who are much more interested in things like homework, marks, and neatness. Ishaan cannot seem to get anything right in class; he is then sent to boarding school, where his life changes forever.
  • For a bit of an older film, I suggest “Sholay”, about a former police officer who enlists the services of two outlaws to capture Gabbar Singh, the notorious and ruthless bandit who murdered his family and has been terrorizing his village.

S. S. Rajamouli (1973)

S. S. Rajamouli's films are characterized by grand narratives that blend mythology, epic storytelling, and larger-than-life characters. He often explores themes of honor, sacrifice, and justice, using spectacular visuals and innovative action sequences to create immersive cinematic experiences that resonate with audiences on both emotional and cultural levels. Here are some recommendations:

  • A movie of his that got some international traction is “RRR”, about a fearless warrior on a perilous mission comes face to face with a steely cop serving British forces in this epic saga set in pre-independent India.
  • This next recommendation is the first part of two movies: “Bāhubali: The Beginning”, in which the young Shivudu is left as a foundling in a small village by his mother. By the time he’s grown up, it has become apparent that he possesses exceptional gifts. He meets the beautiful warrior princess Avanthika and learns that her queen has been held captive for the last 25 years. Shividu sets off to rescue her, discovering his own origins in the process.
  • For a more comedic suggestion I have “Eega”: Nani is a flower decorator, madly in love with his neighbor Bindhu. He gets killed by the baddie, Sudeep, a powerful businessman. Nani comes back as a housefly to get his revenge.

If you are not yet satisfied after these three movies I also recommend “Magadheera” (2009) and “Bāhubali 2: The Conclusion” (2017).

Rajkumar Hirani (1962)

Rajkumar Hirani's films are celebrated for their blend of humor and heartfelt storytelling, often tackling social issues with warmth and optimism. He explores themes of friendship, redemption, and the importance of personal values, using relatable characters and engaging narratives to deliver poignant messages that resonate with audiences. Here are some recommendations:

  • His most popular movie is “3 Idiots”: You’ve never met a college student quite like “Rancho.” From the moment he arrives at India’s most prestigious university, Rancho’s outlandish schemes turn the campus upside down, along with the lives of his two newfound best friends. Together, they make life miserable for “Virus,” the school’s uptight and heartless dean. But when Rancho catches the eye of the dean’s daughter, Virus sets his sights on flunking out the “3 idiots” once and for all.
  • Another recommendation is “PK”, in which an alien on Earth loses the only device he can use to communicate with his spaceship. His innocent nature and child-like questions force the country to evaluate the impact of religious views on people.
  • For more of a deep cut i suggest “Munna Bhai M.B.B.S.”, about a gangster who sets out to fulfill his father’s dream of becoming a doctor.

For more of his movies I recommend: “Lage Raho Munna Bhai” (2006), “Sanju” (2018) and “Dunki” (2023).
